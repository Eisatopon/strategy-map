In many mathematical problems, the main difficulty is not computation, but deciding where to start. A wrong starting point can hide even the right idea.

The Extremal Element strategy is based on a simple principle: start where freedom is minimal. The smallest, largest, first, or last element is the most constrained.

Because of these constraints, the extremal element quickly reveals what cannot happen â€” often enough to solve the problem.

When to use:
  • "At most" / "at least" statements
  • Minimum or maximum conditions
  • Uniqueness arguments
  • One element stands out
Thinking steps:
  1. Identify the extremal element
  2. List its constraints
  3. Analyze its interaction with others
  4. Use restrictions to conclude

Given a finite set of positive integers where all pairwise sums are distinct, prove it contains at most one even number.

Hint: What happens if there are two even numbers? Consider the smallest even number.

ğŸ“™ Intermediate Problem:

Given n points in the plane, prove there exists a point whose removal doesn't decrease the minimal distance.

Hint: Consider the pair achieving minimal distance. Remove a point NOT in this pair.

ğŸ“• Advanced Problem:

In a connected graph, prove there's a vertex whose removal doesn't increase components by more than 1.

Hint: Consider a spanning tree. Which vertex is most constrained? (A leaf!)
