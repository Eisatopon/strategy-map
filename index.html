The hardest part of many mathematical problems is not computation, but deciding where to start. Choosing the wrong starting point can hide even the correct idea.

The Extremal Element strategy is based on a simple principle: start where the freedom is smallest. The smallest, largest, first, or last element is the most constrained â€” and constraints reveal structure.

Understanding what an extremal element cannot do often leads directly to a contradiction or to the desired conclusion.

  • Minimum / maximum conditions

  2. List its constraints
  3. Analyze how it interacts with others
  4. Use the restriction to conclude
